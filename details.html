DID YOU KNOW?

Women make up 49.6% of the world population, women are at the center of the community therefore mobilization, consensus building and coalition building amongst women is essential to create a shift in mindset and norms in our society today.

Their needs experiences, voices and concerns need to be represented at decision making tables

Economic inclusion of women creates opportunity for upward mobility in education, business etc.

Implementation of Conflict resolutions are more effective when women are involved.

It is easy to get involved with us, the more we work together the bigger our collective impact.

2) INTERNSHIP

Gain experience

WWB works with highly motivated individuals excited about transforming the world. Our internship program is designed to provide undergraduates, graduates and post graduates with unique opportunities to develop both hard and soft skills in a friendly and hardworking environment. An internship at WWB is a great opportunity to learn and offer community service by participating and reaching out to women specifically and the society as a whole in areas of peace, human rights and security.

National internship

Internship programs are designed for Cameroonian nationals who are currently attending or who are recent graduates from a university. They are also open to local professionals and qualified individuals with strong interest in peace, security and human rights. It is a great opportunity for current students to gain experience in a professional milieu.

BASIC REQUIREMENTS

● Cameroonian citizenship.
● Willingness to stay for three-six months unless otherwise agreed.
● Ability to work independently within a team and to drive.
● Can work in a multicultural environment.
● Willingness to stay for three-six months, unless otherwise agreed
● Ability to work independently within a team and to drive projects forward with minimal supervision
● Ability and motivation to contribute effectively to both organization’s field work and office responsibility.
● A general understanding of peace, security and human rights issues of women and the desire to know more.
● Adherence to the confidentiality of sensitive information pertaining to WWB work.
● Respect the copyright nature of all written documents prepared by interns, all of which shall be property of WWB.
● Fluency in spoken and written English, knowledge of French is an added advantage.
● Computer literacy

WWB will not pay interns for their office placements and field work. Interns are expected to fund their own transportation, housing and living expenses during the internship period. In order to cover administrative and field work costs, WWB charges an affordable fee for each intern.

International Internship

WWB also encourages international internships. Our international interns will work directly with WWB staff to develop and implement our projects and initiatives. Given the importance of interns to the organization, WWB welcomes applications from different work and educational backgrounds in accordance with the current needs of the organization. An internship with us is a great chance to expand your skills, apply your education and work directly in the field of African peace, security and human rights. It’s also an opportunity to learn from our Cameroonian staff members and constituents/beneficiaries of the organization.

Basic Requirements for International Intern:

● Ability to work independently within a team and to drive projects forward with minimal supervision
● Ability and motivation to contribute effectively to both organization’s field work and office responsibility (both are vital to WWB’s success)
● A general understanding of human rights and the desire to know more
● Willingness to familiarize oneself with all documents relating to the past work of the intern’s particular project.
● Adherence to the confidentiality of sensitive information pertaining to WWB work.
● Respect the copyright nature of all written documents prepared by interns, all of which shall be property of WWB.
● Can work in a multicultural environment.
● Fluency in spoken and written English
● Computer literacy

WWB International Internship Fee:

WWB charges a nominal fee for international internships for better facilitation, coordination, supervision, and effectiveness. Internship prices vary depending on the length of the internship, the project and additional services requested. The internship fees include the costs of housing and work-based transportation. Interns will be responsible for their insurance and visa applications. WWB will assist with a confirmation or visa letter to facilitate the application process.

● We encourage interns that last three months or longer, so that interns have enough time to adjust and to understand the work and context of WWB and its projects.

5) JOIN

WWB strives to create a network of individuals, firms and organizations committed to promoting and protecting peace, security and human rights of women at a local, national and international level. Apart from our projects, our education services and our social justice advocacy, we seek to facilitate such connections through pro-bono partnerships, collaborations and organization membership.

a) Pro-Bono Partnerships

With the insecurity and human rights issues faced by many women in our nation and especially the crisis stricken regions, there is a dire need for affordable legal counsel and we will require some legal assistance. WWB invites any local law firm or individual barrister to join us as a pro-bono partner. If interested please contact our office through email or phone.

b) Collaboration

WWB seeks NGO and other institutions in Cameroon or internationally to collaborate on pertinent projects. Please contact our office through phone or email if your organization seeks to partner with WWB.

c) Membership

Membership with Women Without Border is open to all individuals and groups passionate about women’s human rights, peace and security. Becoming a member of the WWB family will not only endow you with specific membership benefits, but your contribution and support will directly improve the livelihood and wellbeing of many women.

WWB offers:

i) Ordinary membership: open to any individual who supports the mission of WWB. The ordinary members constitute the bulk of the WWB members and are required to pay an annual subscription fee.

ii) Group membership: those eligible for group membership include communities, clubs, civil society organizations, schools, unions and other bodies of more than one individual willing to support and foster the work of WWB. Groups present reports of their activities to the Board of Directors and participate in the WWB decision making process. Each group is required to pay an annual subscription fee.

iii) Board Membership: Board members fund the activities of WWB. Their subscription goes to cover rents and maintenance, and a minimum stipend for staff and volunteers in the absence of outside funding. Board members directly oversee the smooth operation of the Executive Secretariat and meet in extraordinary sessions to take major decisions of the organization.
